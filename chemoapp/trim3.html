Trimester 3

Week 28

Your baby is the size of a cucumber. Dreaming of baby? Your baby is dreaming too, as well as blinking, coughing and hiccupping.

Week 29

Your baby is the size of an extra large bottle of water. Your baby's packing on the pounds, which look so cute on her. If only you could say the same for your varicose veins...

Week 30

Your baby is the size of a cabbage. Your baby's brain is getting smarter by the minute, but for Mom, the only thing that smarts right now may be heartburn pain.

Week 31

Your baby is the size of a butternut squash. As your baby's senses increase, your breathing room decreases.

Week 32

Your baby is the size of half a gallon of milk. Your baby is practicing survival skills like sucking and breathing, while your uterus is practicing some Braxton Hicks contractions.

Week 33

Your baby is the size of a head of lettuce. Your baby's immune system gets a boost, while her sleepless mommy could use a boost of energy.

Week 34

Your baby is the size of a honeydew. A boy baby's testicles descend this week (whoopee!), but Mom should also look out below — and above — as her vision becomes less sharp.

Week 35

Your baby is the size of a pineapple. A rapidly growing brain makes your baby's head weigh more, which means more pressure on Mommy's bladder.

Week 36

Your baby is the size of a canary melon. Your baby's bones may be ready to rock and roll, but yours may be aching right now.

Week 37

Your baby is the size of a large cantaloupe. Your doctor may check for labor signs, while your baby prepares for birth by sucking, turning and breathing in the womb.

Week 38

Your baby is the size of a winter melon. Your baby is producing surfactant, which will help him take those first breaths, while you're producing colostrum, the precursor to breast milk.

Week 39

Your baby is the size of a small pumpkin. Your baby's brain development is still in high gear, even as you may start to experience some definite signs of labor.

Week 40

Your baby is the size of a watermelon. It's the official end of your pregnancy — though your baby might not have gotten that memo. Hang in there!

Week 41

Your baby is the size of a jackfruit. It's not unusual for a baby to be overdue — and you to feel overdone — but you're apt to feel real labor pains anytime this week or next.

YOU ARE A MAMA.   CONGRATULATIONS!!!

Week 42

Your baby is the size of a pumpkin. Most babies have their own timetables when it comes to delivery; fewer than 5 percent are actually born on their due date.
